SafeVLA — Constraining Robots
Toward Safe-by-Default Behavior

A vision-language-action (VLA) model is fine-tuned inside a constrained Markov decision process: safety violations become cost signals, and a Lagrangian multiplier automatically tightens the policy whenever cumulative cost creeps above budget — trading a little task reward for a lot of physical safety.

Stage 0 / 5 — idle
(A) MODEL
🏷️

Task Modeling

Defines each task as (scene, position, orientation, objects, Φ, Ψ) — formal state-action and trajectory safety predicates.

(B) ELICIT
🏚️

Risk Elicitation

150K ProcTHOR homes + 800K Objaverse objects, seeded with 5 hazard patterns, generate trajectories in AI2THOR.

PREDICATES
⚠️

Cost Translation

φ (state-action) violations cost 1 at the bad step; ψ (trajectory) violations cost 1 at the final step of the bad segment.

(C) CONSTRAIN
⚖️

Lagrangian CMDP

min–max optimization: reward maximized subject to cost ≤ budget. Multiplier λ rises when cost is over budget, relaxes when safe.

(D) ASSURE
🔍

Safety Assurance

Held-out + OOD test-time safety, long-tail cost analysis, and deliberately unsolvable "extreme failure" probes.

↻ Updated policy πθi+1 re-enters (B) to generate new trajectories — the loop repeats until convergence.
