# SafeDojo Architecture

## 🎯 Purpose
SafeDojo addresses the limitations of traditional safety testing by building an **Interactive World Model** into the training loop for Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policies. Its goal is to train a policy that *proactively* avoids dangerous states and actions, rather than just reacting when they occur—all while doing so without physically running risky rollouts.

## ⚙️ Core Methodology
The system combines three advanced machine learning components:

### 1. Interactive World Model
*   **Role:** Acts as the virtual training environment. It predicts the next frame $(\hat{o}_{t+1})$ given the current observation, the action, and the language instruction.
*   **Model:** An action-conditioned video predictor $p_\phi$, built on a large open video diffusion/DiT model (Wan 2.2).
*   **Conditioning:** The predicted frame is conditioned on:
    1.  A historical observation ($\hat{o}_{i,k}$).
    2.  The action chunk ($a_{i,k}$), which is visually projected onto the image plane as an *action map*.
    3.  The language instruction ($l$).
*   **Key Improvement:** The model employs **static-video augmentation**. By periodically freezing conditioning frames during training, it combats *exposure error*, preventing the model from becoming overly reliant on perfect self-generated observations—a common failure point in world models.

### 2. Dual-Branch Reward/Cost Evaluator
SafeDojo departs significantly from single-reward optimization by maintaining two separate, independently trained heads applied to every imagined rollout:

*   **Task Success Classifier ($f_\text{task}$):** A ResNet-style binary classifier that predicts per-frame task progress based on the RGB frames. This yields a dense, continuous task reward $r_{i,h}$.
*   **Collision Risk Classifier ($f_\text{safe}$):** A specialized latent encoder/MLP that operates only on the *latent features* generated by the world model (not the raw pixels). It predicts per-step obstacle contact probability $c_{i,h}$.

### 3. Lagrangian Constrained GRPO Optimization
The core training loop combines these two signals:

*   **Reward Aggregation:** The task reward is calculated as a simple average over all steps.
*   **Cost Aggregation (Key Distinction):** The safety cost is aggregated using a **top-$M$ average** of the highest-risk steps ($M=16$). This deliberately focuses the optimization on the most dangerous *moments*, rather than averaging the risk across the entire trajectory, which might dilute critical violations.
*   **Constraint Integration:** It uses **Lagrangian Constrained Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO)**. Instead of a fixed penalty $\lambda$, it updates an adaptive Lagrange multiplier ($\eta$) every batch based on whether the *average* safety cost exceeds a defined safety budget $d$. This dynamic adjustment allows the constraint pressure to self-adjust, providing more stable and natural adherence to safety budgets than static methods.

## 📚 Data Efficiency & Evaluation
*   **Benchmark:** SafeLIBERO (a collision-augmented version of LIBERO).
*   **Claimed Performance:** Achieves state-of-the-art safe-success rates on multiple suites, demonstrating generalization ability from Level I training to Level II (zero-shot) testing.
*   **Training Data:** The world model is trained on 1.5K trajectories augmented with self-generated rollouts, ensuring the system learns what "failure looks like."
