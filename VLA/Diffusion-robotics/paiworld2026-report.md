# PAIWorld: A 3D-Consistent World Foundation Model for Robotic Manipulation

## TL;DR

PAIWorld (The PAIWorld Team, Institute of AI for Industries, Chinese Academy of Sciences) fixes a specific failure mode in multi-view world models for robot manipulation: when a diffusion world model has to generate future video from several cameras at once (egocentric, eye-to-hand, wrist), naively concatenating each view's tokens produces cross-view object drift, depth contradictions, and texture misalignment, because the model has no explicit way to exchange information between views and no signal telling it what "3D-consistent" content even looks like. The paper argues these two deficiencies must be fixed together, not separately, and builds three components on top of a DiT-based flow-matching backbone (Cosmos-Predict2.5) to do so: **Geometry-Aware Cross-View Attention** and **Geometric Rotary Position Embedding (Geo-RoPE)** open and geometrically bias an inter-view communication pathway, while **Latent 3D-REPA** distills relational 3D structure from a frozen 3D foundation model (Depth Anything 3) to supervise what flows through that pathway. The central empirical claim — that the two remedies are individually modest but jointly super-additive — is directly tested in an ablation and holds up. PAIWorld ranks 1st on the WorldArena leaderboard, 2nd on AgiBot-Challenge2026, and wins 6 of 7 metrics on the AgiBot-World text-conditioned benchmark, with its clearest wins concentrated specifically on 3D/geometric-consistency metrics.

## Architecture

**Backbone.** PAIWorld builds on **Cosmos-Predict2.5**, a ~14B-parameter flow-matching Diffusion Transformer (DiT) operating in the latent space of a pretrained spatial-temporal video VAE (following Wan2.1's ST-VAE for spatial+temporal compression). Standard flow matching: a velocity field `u_θ(z_s, s)` is trained to transport samples along the linear interpolation path `z_s = (1−s)z_0 + sε` from noise to data, minimizing `E[‖u_θ(z_s,s) − (ε−z_0)‖²]`. Text conditioning (via Cosmos-Reason1 embeddings) is injected through AdaLN; action conditioning follows EVAC by rendering actions as **spatial action maps** concatenated with the noisy latent along the channel dimension, rather than encoding actions as abstract vectors — this grounds action semantics directly in pixel space (e.g., an end-effector trajectory projected into each camera view).

**The problem with the naive baseline.** Flat multi-view token concatenation — stacking all views' tokens along the sequence axis and running standard temporal self-attention over the result — treats multi-view tokens exactly like temporal tokens. There's no inductive bias telling the model which tokens across different views correspond to the same 3D point; it has to discover that purely from data, which the paper argues becomes unreliable as view count and scene complexity grow.

**Component 1 — Geometric Rotary Position Embedding (Geo-RoPE).** Each attention head's query/key vectors are split into two equal subspaces, each independently rotated via RoPE:
- **Ray subspace:** for a token at pixel `(h,w)` in view `v`, the world-space ray direction is computed by unprojecting the pixel through the camera intrinsics and rotating by the inverse camera rotation: `d^v(h,w) = normalize((R^v)ᵀ·(K^v)⁻¹·[h+0.5, w+0.5, 1]ᵀ)`. This becomes the RoPE position for the ray subspace, so tokens observing the same 3D point — even from different cameras — receive similar rotations.
- **Pose subspace:** a 12-dimensional per-view feature (Euler angles, translation, camera position, optical axis) is shared across all spatial positions within that view and rotates the pose subspace.
- Splitting the two prevents the spatially-varying ray signal from interfering with the spatially-uniform pose signal.

**Component 2 — Geometry-Aware Cross-View Attention.** At selected DiT layers, a dedicated cross-view attention sub-block lets each view's queries attend to *all* views' keys/values — but only after the queries and keys have been Geo-RoPE-rotated using each view's own camera geometry. Because of this, a query token from view `v` and a key token from view `v′` get a high inner product precisely when they observe the same 3D point, so geometrically corresponding tokens naturally attract higher attention weight — the geometric bias is built into the attention pattern itself, not learned from scratch. The block is gated with **AdaLN-Zero** (gate initialized to zero), so the pretrained single-view backbone is exactly preserved at initialization and the new pathway contributes only gradually as training proceeds. Periodically, this is complemented by a **spatial-concat self-attention** variant that flattens view and spatial dimensions into one long token axis for a broader joint receptive field.
- *Why this alone isn't enough:* an architectural pathway only shapes how information *can* flow — it doesn't specify what content is 3D-consistent. Without a geometric objective, the pathway can still minimize the training loss via shortcuts like copying textures across views or averaging features, which the paper argues is exactly what tends to happen in practice.

**Component 3 — Latent 3D-REPA (the geometric objective).** A frozen **Depth Anything 3** (DA3) model — trained under direct 3D geometric supervision (depth, point maps, camera poses) — supplies the target features. Rather than regressing DA3's absolute features token-by-token (which would be confounded by the feature-space gap between the two encoders), 3D-REPA distills **relational structure**: the pairwise cosine-similarity pattern between tokens. Since computing a full token-token similarity matrix is quadratic and prohibitive, it uses **anchor sampling** — a random subset of `K` tokens serves as anchors, and every token's similarity to just those anchors is computed (`O(MK)` instead of `O(M²)`). This relational-similarity alignment is applied at two granularities and summed: `L_spatial` (within a single frame, across views and space) and `L_temporal` (across the whole clip). Both DA3 and the projection head mapping DiT features into DA3's feature space participate; DA3 itself stays frozen throughout training, and also supplies the camera extrinsics/intrinsics that Geo-RoPE uses.
- *Why this alone isn't enough:* a per-frame geometric prior sharpens each view's own 3D awareness in isolation, but without a cross-view pathway there's no way for that improved awareness to propagate into agreement *across* views.

**Why the combination is claimed to be non-additive.** Geo-RoPE fixes a shared 3D coordinate frame across views; Cross-View Attention opens the pathway that routes information along that frame; 3D-REPA supervises the content moving through the pathway to be geometrically faithful. Because Geo-RoPE has already put every view into a common reference frame, 3D-REPA's constraints propagate coherently across views instead of collapsing into isolated per-view fixes — each remedy addresses a failure mode the other cannot, so the paper's claim is that their value only emerges jointly. Total training objective: `L_total = L_diff + λ·L_REPA`, with `λ = 0.5`.

## Data

~2.5M multi-view robotic-manipulation video clips curated from five sources: AgiBot-World (35%), RoboMIND (20%), Galaxea (15%), RoboTwin (15%), and RoboCOIN (15%) — spanning diverse robot embodiments, manipulation tasks, and camera configurations, each with text descriptions and/or action annotations. For the action-conditioned evaluation specifically, the model is further fine-tuned on task-specific data from the AgiBot-Challenge2026 and WorldArena benchmarks.

## Training setup

200 NVIDIA H200 GPUs, 30,000 iterations (~7 days total), AdamW optimizer, cosine learning-rate schedule with linear warmup over the first 3,000 iterations to a peak learning rate of `3×10⁻⁵`. REPA loss weight `λ = 0.5`. Depth Anything 3 is kept entirely frozen throughout training, serving as a fixed geometric prior; the Cross-View Attention blocks use AdaLN-Zero gating so training starts from an exactly-preserved pretrained single-view backbone. The REPA projection head is randomly initialized; Cross-View Attention and Geo-RoPE modules are inserted into the pretrained DiT backbone.

## Evaluation & results

- **WorldArena (action-conditioned, 7 metrics, Table 1):** PAIWorld ranks **1st overall**, EWMScore 72.31 — narrowly ahead of UNIS (72.16) and BWM-Fast (72.15) — with the 2nd-best Motion Quality score (80.45). The paper's framing: competing methods each excel on a narrow subset of the seven axes (e.g., WorldScape tops Physics Adherence but collapses on Motion Quality at 42.34; UNIS leads Motion Quality and Controllability but trails on Content Consistency and 3D Accuracy), whereas PAIWorld is the only entry that stays near the top across every axis simultaneously.
- **AgiBot-Challenge2026 (action-conditioned, 4 metrics, Table 2):** PAIWorld ranks **2nd overall** (EWMScore 0.8245, behind NeoVerse-ABot's 0.829) but achieves the **best Scene Consistency** (0.9041) of any entry — the metric most directly tied to the paper's stated design objective. Its nDTW score (0.9531) indicates generated trajectories track the ground-truth action sequence closely, validating the spatial-action-map conditioning choice.
- **AgiBot-World (text-conditioned multi-view, 7 metrics, Table 3, vs. Genie-Envisioner / Cosmos-Predict2 / Wan2.1):** PAIWorld wins **6 of 7 metrics** — SSIM 0.7683 (+3.2% over the runner-up), LPIPS 0.1844 (−45%), FID 45.04 (−20% vs. Wan2.1), FVD 175.78, and — most tellingly for the paper's core claim — the best **MEt3R** score (14.20, a −10% improvement, a metric that directly measures multi-view 3D reconstruction error) and best **Geometric** consistency score (0.4056). It loses only on Scene Consistency (0.9041 vs. Genie-Envisioner's 0.9231), which the authors attribute to Genie-Envisioner's dedicated text-grounding mechanism.
- **Ablation (Table 4, AgiBot-World, MEt3R lower=better):** backbone alone = 16.84; + Cross-View-Attention/Geo-RoPE alone → 15.91 (Δ0.93); + 3D-REPA alone → 16.12 (Δ0.72); both together → 14.20 (**Δ2.64**) — exceeding the naive sum of the individual gains (0.93+0.72=1.65). SSIM/LPIPS/FID all improve most when both components are present together, not just MEt3R.

## Limitations (as stated by the authors)

The paper has no section explicitly labeled "Limitations," but its Conclusion's "future work" discussion names what the current system does not yet do:

- **No physics beyond geometry.** Current 3D consistency is purely geometric; contact dynamics, deformable objects, and fluid simulation are explicitly named as unaddressed, future "physics-aware" extensions.
- **No long-horizon consistency mechanism.** The paper states that scaling to long-horizon planning "will require maintaining 3D consistency over extended temporal rollouts, potentially through hierarchical or recurrent architectures" — i.e., the current architecture doesn't yet have machinery for this.
- **The world-model/policy self-improvement loop is aspirational, not implemented.** The described closed loop — world model generates experience, a World Action Model (WAM) learns from it, the improved policy collects better real data, which refines the world model — is stated as a future direction ("we aim to build"), not something evaluated in this paper.

## Critical read

**What's genuinely novel:** the diagnosis and the empirical demonstration that it's correct. Plenty of papers claim two components are "complementary"; this one directly tests that claim with a controlled ablation (Table 4) and gets a genuinely super-additive result (2.64 vs. an additive prediction of 1.65), with a specific mechanistic story for *why* (Geo-RoPE's shared reference frame is what lets 3D-REPA's per-view constraints propagate coherently instead of collapsing into isolated fixes). That's a more rigorous piece of evidence for a "both parts are structurally necessary" claim than most papers making a similar synergy argument provide.

**What's a fairly direct transplant of existing techniques:** each individual component leans heavily on prior work. Geo-RoPE is rotary position embedding (Su et al.) applied to camera geometry, in a similar spirit to CameraCtrl's Plücker-coordinate camera conditioning (cited by the paper itself) — the specific ray/pose dual-subspace split is a real design choice, but "encode geometry via rotary embeddings" is not new. Latent 3D-REPA is an extension of the REPA framework (Yu et al., 2025, cited directly) from 2D image diffusion to multi-view video, swapping in Depth Anything 3 features as the alignment target; the relational (anchor-sampled similarity) reformulation instead of absolute-feature regression is the specific new twist, motivated by the feature-space gap between the DiT and DA3 — but "distill a frozen geometry foundation model's features into a generative model" is conceptually continuous with existing REPA-style work. The Cross-View Attention block's zero-init-gated insertion into a pretrained backbone is a standard recipe from the DiT/ControlNet-style conditioning literature; the specific contribution is combining that gating recipe with Geo-RoPE-rotated queries/keys so the *attention pattern itself* carries the geometric bias.

**Open questions / places the results are softer than the headline suggests:**
- PAIWorld is not the outright best on every benchmark: it's 2nd overall on AgiBot-Challenge2026 and loses on Scene Consistency in the text-conditioned setting. The paper's consistent response — "we win on the metrics most tied to 3D consistency, our actual design objective" — is a reasonable and largely well-supported framing, but it is also the framing a paper would use whenever it isn't outright first, so it's worth reading with a bit of skepticism rather than taking at face value.
- **EWMScore's exact aggregation formula across the seven WorldArena sub-metrics is never given in the paper.** The claim that PAIWorld wins because of "balance across metrics" rather than any single dominant axis is plausible given the sub-metric table, but without the actual weighting formula, a reader can't independently verify that "balance" is what the aggregate score is actually rewarding.
- **The three downstream applications the Conclusion credits to PAIWorld's improved consistency — model-based planning, world action model fine-tuning, and multi-view policy post-training — are asserted, not demonstrated.** No quantitative results for any of the three appear anywhere in the paper; they're claimed benefits of the approach, not measured ones, in this text.
- **The ablation (Table 4) is run on only one of the three benchmarks (AgiBot-World).** It's the cleanest evidence the paper offers for the two components' individual and joint contributions, but it doesn't establish whether the same super-additive pattern holds on WorldArena or AgiBot-Challenge2026 specifically.
- **Depth Anything 3 is itself a very recent, contemporaneous model** (cited as an ICLR 2026 paper). PAIWorld's geometric supervision is only as correct as DA3's own depth/pose estimates in this specific robotic-manipulation domain, which the paper doesn't independently verify — the 3D "ground truth" being distilled is itself a learned estimate, not measured geometry.
