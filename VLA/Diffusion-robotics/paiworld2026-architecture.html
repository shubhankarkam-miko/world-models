PAIWorld: a pathway and an objective for multi-view 3D consistency

Multi-view world models drift, warp, and misalign objects across cameras when views generate in isolation. PAIWorld's claim: fixing this needs two things at once — a way for views to exchange information, and a geometric signal telling that exchange what "correct" looks like. Neither alone is enough; the diagram below follows one prediction step through both.

Inside Geo-RoPE: why queries and keys are split in two

Every attention head's query and key vectors are split into two equal halves before rotation, so a fine-grained per-pixel signal and a coarse per-view signal can't interfere with each other.

ray subspace (half the dimensions)

Rotated using the token's own 3D ray direction — computed by unprojecting its pixel through that camera's intrinsics and rotating by the inverse camera rotation. Varies per pixel. This is what makes a token in one view and the corresponding token in another view rotate similarly when they observe the same 3D point.

pose subspace (the other half)

Rotated using a single 12-dimensional vector per view — Euler angles, translation, camera position, optical axis. Identical for every pixel in that view. This carries "which camera is this" without competing against the pixel-level ray signal.

Why both pillars: the ablation

MEt3R directly measures multi-view 3D reconstruction error — lower is better. Adding either component alone helps a little; adding both helps far more than the two individual gains added together.

0.93 + 0.72 = 1.65 improvement expected if the two components simply added — the measured joint improvement is 2.64. The dashed line marks where an additive combination would land.
