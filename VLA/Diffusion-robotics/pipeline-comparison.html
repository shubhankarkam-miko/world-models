Schematic — Paper A vs. Paper B

Two ways to let RL touch a diffusion planner

Both papers put a diffusion model in charge of proposing trajectories. They disagree on when the reinforcement-learning signal is allowed to act on that proposal — after it's finished, or at every single step while it's still being generated. Step through each pipeline to see where.

Paper A — generate once, select once Paper B — generate while steering, every step — dashed border = the block where the RL/MARL signal actually acts —
PAPER A

Generate → Select

Diffusion proposes K candidate sub-paths for the upcoming horizon window; a SAC policy scores the finished set and picks one. The diffusion process itself never sees the RL signal.

PAPER B

Generate ← Guided

Each robot denoises its own trajectory independently, but a centralized MARL critic's gradient is injected into every agent's denoising mean at every reverse-diffusion step — before the trajectory is finished.
