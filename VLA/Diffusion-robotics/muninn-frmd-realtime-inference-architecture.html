Paper deep dive · two papers, one bottleneck

Two paths to real-time diffusion for robots

Both papers attack the same problem — iterative denoising makes diffusion-based trajectory/action generation too slow for real-time control — from opposite ends of the training-cost spectrum. Muninn wraps a pretrained model with a certified caching policy, no retraining. FRMD retrains the sampler away entirely, collapsing it into one forward pass.

How one step's decision is made

    Probe Ψ (cheap stem)

  3. 3
    Sampler sensitivity L_t
    analytic — how much an error here gets amplified later
  4. 4
    Conformal bound U(s_t)
    calibrated, distribution-free upper bound on reuse error

    Budget check
    would spending this cost exceed B_rem?
  6. 6
    Reuse or recompute
    spend the budget, or pay full compute this step
